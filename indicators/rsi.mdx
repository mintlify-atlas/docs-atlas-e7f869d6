## What is RSI?

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) is a momentum oscillator that measures the speed and magnitude of recent price changes to evaluate overbought or oversold conditions in the XAUUSD market.

<Info>
RSI values range from 0 to 100, with readings above 70 indicating overbought conditions and below 30 indicating oversold conditions.
</Info>

## How RSI Works

RSI calculates the ratio of upward price movements to downward price movements over a specified period (typically 14 periods):

- **RSI = 100 - (100 / (1 + RS))**
- **RS = Average Gain / Average Loss**

The indicator smooths price data to reveal momentum trends that may not be obvious from raw price action.

## RSI in the Trading System

The XAUUSD Trading Assistant AI uses RSI across multiple timeframes to:

### 1. Identify Momentum Shifts

<Tip>
When RSI crosses above 50, it signals bullish momentum. Crossing below 50 indicates bearish momentum.
</Tip>

The AI monitors RSI on all six timeframes (D1, H4, H1, M30, M15, M5) to detect momentum alignment:

- **All timeframes RSI > 50**: Strong bullish confluence
- **All timeframes RSI < 50**: Strong bearish confluence
- **Mixed RSI readings**: Choppy market, wait for clarity

### 2. Detect Divergences

Divergences between price and RSI often signal potential reversals:

**Bullish Divergence**:
- Price makes lower lows
- RSI makes higher lows
- Signal: Potential bullish reversal

**Bearish Divergence**:
- Price makes higher highs
- RSI makes lower highs
- Signal: Potential bearish reversal

<Note>
Divergences are most reliable on H1 and H4 timeframes in the XAUUSD market.
</Note>

### 3. Confirm Trend Strength

RSI helps validate trend quality:

- **Strong Uptrend**: RSI stays above 40, rarely touching 30
- **Strong Downtrend**: RSI stays below 60, rarely touching 70
- **Weak Trend**: RSI frequently crosses 50 line

## RSI Trading Strategies

### Strategy 1: Overbought/Oversold Reversals

**Long Setup**:
- RSI drops below 30 on M15 or M5
- Higher timeframes (H1, H4) show uptrend
- Look for RSI to cross back above 30 for entry

**Short Setup**:
- RSI rises above 70 on M15 or M5
- Higher timeframes (H1, H4) show downtrend
- Look for RSI to cross back below 70 for entry

### Strategy 2: RSI 50 Line Crossover

**Bullish Signal**:
- RSI crosses above 50 on H1 or H4
- Confirms shift to bullish momentum
- Enter on pullback to support

**Bearish Signal**:
- RSI crosses below 50 on H1 or H4
- Confirms shift to bearish momentum
- Enter on pullback to resistance

## Common RSI Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Trading Overbought/Oversold Blindly**: RSI can stay extreme during strong trends
2. **Ignoring Higher Timeframes**: M5 RSI oversold means little if D1 is in strong downtrend
3. **Missing Divergences**: These are powerful reversal signals often overlooked
4. **Using RSI Alone**: Always combine with price action and other indicators

<Note>
The AI avoids these mistakes by analyzing RSI in context with all other indicators and timeframes.
</Note>
